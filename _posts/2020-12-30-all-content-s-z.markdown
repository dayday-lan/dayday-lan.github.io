### Sage
* Symbol of Wisdom and Immortality, Vice and Virtue.
<br>

* The ancient Romans and Greeks believed that it represented domestic virtue and satyrs, mythical half-goat, half-men that loved debauchery and drinking at the same time.

* Pagan practitioners use sage brooms to cleanse negative energies.
<br>

# Salvia, Blue
* The blue flowers of a Salvia are often aligned with friendship and family and symbolic of thoughtful gestures such as sentiments of “missing you” or “thinking of you”.	
<br>

![]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/Salvia, blue.jpg)
<br>

# Salvia, Red
* The red blooming Salvia flower is traditionally associated with love and romance and is symbolic of “Forever mine” gestures.

* Salvias make thoughtful gifts for a variety of occasions.
![]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/Salvia, red.jpg)

# Savory	
* The savory herb held the symbolic meaning of love and romance.

* White Savory: honesty and sympathy.

* Pink Savory: Happiness and gentleness.

* Lilac Savory: convey wealth, good fortune, and new beginnings.
<br>

# Snapdragon	
* Purple snapdragons symbolize spirituality, which is different from other purple flowers that represent royalty.

* Red snapdragons are associated with passion and love.

* White snapdragons represent purity, innocence and grace.

* Yellow snapdragons symbolize happiness and good luck.
<br>

![]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/Snapdragon.jpg)

# Southernwood	
* White flowers: purity, peace, healing, clarity, and even holiness.

* Yellow and cream-colored flowers: joy, growth, youthfulness, fun, and exuberance.
<br>

* Since it was commonly given by young people first starting a relationship, it’s not as passionate or intense as flowers like red roses.

# Tulip
* Red: undying love, Also, the bloom says, “trust me or believe me.” In some contexts, it may also mean charity or fame.

* Pink: “You’re my perfect lover.”

* Purple: eternal love.

* Orange: “I’m fascinated by you.”

* White: sincerity or forgiveness, which makes them the best apology flowers.

* Yellow: “There’s a sunshine in my smile.” It represents friendship, but can also represent hopeless love or no chance of reconciliation.

* Black: sacrificial love. It’s the best flower of choice for your first bouquet to give to someone you admire. They’re regarded as the 11th wedding anniversary flower too.
<br>

![]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/Tulip.jpg)

# Yarrow
* The yarrow flower shares twin meanings of healing and love.

* White: Renewal, peace, purity, new beginnings, hope, and clarity.

* Pink: Love, happiness, joy, youthfulness, and fun.

* Red: Passion, romantic, courage, power, and strength.

* Yellow: Friendship, warmth, excitement, celebration, and positivity.

* Orange: Enthusiasm, fresh starts, appreciation, gratitude, and friendship.

<br>

# Zinnia
* Green: Growth, wealth, money, success, and new starts.

* Yellow: Joyfulness, energy, youthfulness, friendship, and support.

* White: Purity, healing, recovery, peace, and elegance.

* Orange: Power, energy, success, drive, ambition, and family bonds.

* Red: Romantic love, passion, health, success, and good health.

* Pink: Happiness, gentleness, grace, gentleness, and non-romantic love.

* Purple: Elegance, regalness, royalty, wealth, success, and commitment.
